Q: What is the Security deposit/Bank guarantee/SBG?
A: The Security Deposit, Bank Guarantee, or SBG refers to a financial assurance mechanism in contracts, typically amounting to 5% of the contract value, aimed at ensuring the contractor's compliance and performance under the contract terms. It can be submitted as cash, term deposits, or irrevocable bank guarantees from scheduled commercial banks, and serves as a form of protection for the entity engaging the contractor.

Q: What are the Penalty clauses, if any: check if there are any penalty clauses?
A: The penalty clauses include: 1. **Measurement Errors**: For exaggerated or false measurements: - First occurrence: 10% penalty on the claimed gross bill. - Subsequent occurrences: 15% penalty and withdrawal of measurement recording rights. 2. **Liquidated Damages**: If work is delayed beyond contract time, penalties range from 0.05% to 0.30% of the contract value per week, up to a maximum of 5%. 3. **Performance Guarantee**: If the contractor fails to submit the guarantee on time, the contract may be terminated, and the bid security forfeited. 4. **Contract Rescission**: In case of contract default, the Railway may forfeit the security deposit . 5. **Interest on Advances**: Misuse of advances results in a breach, allowing for contract termination and forfeiture.
